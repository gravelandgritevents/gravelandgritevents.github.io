What on earth is a Backyard Ultra?

The format is simple. The challenge is not.

Every hour, on the hour, runners complete one 4.167 mile loop. Finish in time and you earn the right to go again. Miss the bell, finish late, or choose not to continue, and your race is over.

There is no fixed distance, no traditional finish line and no set race duration. The last runner standing is the only finisher.

Blaise Castle Estate

Beautiful, accessible, and demanding

The loop winds through woodland trails, open fields, and rocky sections, particularly the technical descent down Dynamite Hill. Expect around 160m of elevation gain each lap, with terrain that rewards patience more than speed.

This is not a fast, flat Backyard Ultra. The Bristol Backyard Ultra is a brutally honest course, designed to showcase the beautiful Blaise Castle Estate. This is a characterful, hilly, and at times technical route. A proper Bristol challenge, built around grit and community celebration.

Quick answers

Common questions before you enter

Is it beginner friendly?

Yes, because you choose how far you go. But the Blaise Castle course is hilly, technical and deserves respect.

Do I need a crew?

No. Plenty of runners take part solo, and we will again provide a Crewless Crew area at basecamp.

Can friends and family come?

Absolutely. Supporters are part of the atmosphere, and Blaise Castle Estate has space and facilities nearby.

What should I bring?

Trail shoes, warm layers, waterproofs, spare socks, headtorch, nutrition, hydration and basecamp comfort kit.
